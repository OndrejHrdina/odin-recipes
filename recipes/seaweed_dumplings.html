Seaweed Dumplings

This is one of the gems of traditional sindolan cuisine. Seaweed dumplings are made from local nutrient-rich seaweed and soft marshwheat.

Ingredients

  • 2 handfuls of speckled seaweed
  • 5 handfuls of whole marshwheat flour
  • a cup of brackish water
  • a pinch of rainbow pepper

Steps

  1. Mix the flour and wheat in a bowl
  2. Let it rest for half an hour, uncovered
  3. Mix the seaweed and the pepper Beware:certain rainbow pepper farms produce really spice pepper!
  4. Divide the dough into 6 pieces, then form them into balls
  5. Roll the doughballs into thin circles Do it quick, so they don't dry up!
  6. Add a spoonful of seaweed mix onto each circle
  7. Close the circles by adding a bit of water to their edges and sealing them shut, forming a half-moon shape
  8. Drop formed dumplings into boiling water and boil them for 5 minutes
  9. Enjoy!
